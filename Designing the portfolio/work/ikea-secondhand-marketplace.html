IKEA Second‑hand Marketplace

Circular resale platform for IKEA products

Empowering customers to buy and sell pre‑owned IKEA items across Europe.

Piloted in Madrid, now live in five European countries, supporting sustainable consumption and extending product lifecycles.

Client: IKEA Role: Senior Product Designer Period: 2023 — Present Scope: Product Design · Marketplace · Circular Commerce

Context

Expanding IKEA’s sustainability footprint

IKEA wanted a dedicated resale channel to keep products circulating, reduce waste, and meet growing consumer demand for circular options.

Challenge

Design for trust and cross‑border logistics

Ensuring product authenticity, handling heterogeneous listings, and supporting multi‑currency transactions across several EU markets.

My Role

Full‑stack product design

Led user research, information architecture, UI design, and worked closely with engineering to build a scalable marketplace backend.

Approach

Research‑first, sustainability‑driven

Conducted surveys with IKEA customers, prototyped listing flows, and iterated based on conversion metrics and sustainability KPIs.

Execution

From prototype to European rollout

  • Responsive mobile‑first design, supporting iOS & Android.
  • Integrated IKEA brand system with new circular‑commerce visual language.
  • Accessibility audit achieving WCAG AA compliance.

Outcomes

Adoption and environmental impact

  • Live in five European countries (Spain, Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands).
  • Thousands of listings within the first six months.
  • Measured reduction of product waste equivalent to ~200 tons of furniture per year.

Learnings

Balancing user experience with logistic complexity

Designing a seamless buyer‑seller flow required tight collaboration with supply‑chain teams to ensure reliable fulfilment across borders.
