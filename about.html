About

I've always been more comfortable
with hard problems than easy briefs.

I'm Jose Romero — a senior product designer with 12 years of experience across digital products, conversion strategy, and design systems. I work in English and Spanish at professional level, and I'm currently based in Zaragoza, Spain, working as an embedded senior designer on IKEA's New Business Platform — part of the team building two peer-to-peer marketplaces from their early stages through European expansion.

Before that, I spent three years at Flat 101, one of Spain's leading digital consultancies, running UX and CRO work across four to eight concurrent enterprise accounts simultaneously. Santander Nordics, Meliá Hotels International, Mapfre, Mutua Madrileña, AXA Spain, IQOS, XTI — different industries, different team structures, often at the same time.

That arc — over 120 projects spanning print, identity, digital product, and CRO, then a sharp narrowing into harder, longer, more complex engagements, and now full embeddedness inside a single product team — was not planned. But looking back, each phase earned the conditions for the next.

IKEA Home Services

A peer-to-peer marketplace connecting homeowners with verified professionals across 50+ home improvement categories. Launched in Madrid in September 2024, expanded to seven Spanish cities by mid-2026. Published figures: 500,000+ downloads, 21,000 service requests completed, 57,000 new or reactivated IKEA Family members, 4.9/5 customer satisfaction rating. I work across user flows, prototyping, in-store user research sessions at IKEA locations, and accessibility auditing — alongside product owners, service designers, engineers, content designers, and business developers.

Peer-to-peer resale of IKEA products, with IKEA's catalog as a data backbone for listings. Piloted in Madrid, now live in five European countries, with full European expansion committed. I contribute to product design and cross-functional collaboration as the platform matures and scales.

Design operations

Built the team's ways-of-working system from scratch — a cross-tool framework spanning Figma (source of truth), Jira, Confluence, and Phrase — replacing an informal, person-dependent process with something documented, teachable, and designed to survive personnel change.

AI workflow design & facilitation

Facilitating Shape Up methodology sessions for the product team — and designing the AI layer on top. The facilitation work led to a parallel body of work: designing AI-assisted workflows for the shaping process itself, including multi-agent orchestration approaches that distribute complex product features across specialized agents (problem decomposition, constraint mapping, solution evaluation). Outputs are structured to flow directly into the team's established Confluence documentation format.

Running AI literacy sessions for senior design professionals at Garaje de Ideas, focused on workflow design rather than tool use.

Factor Común
2016 — 2021
Co-founder & Design Lead
Havana, Cuba

I co-founded Factor Común at 23 — a registered design and communications studio in Havana — while completing a five-year degree in Visual Communication Design. What started as a freelance collaboration with two colleagues grew into a formal studio covering brand identity, editorial design, digital interfaces, audiovisual production, and campaign design, for institutional clients across Cuba.

Running that studio meant handling more than design: client relationships, project planning, supplier coordination, and new business — across disciplines and under significant resource constraints. The scale was different from anything I've done since, but the operational principles were the same. It gave me a foundation in structured thinking and commercial reality that preceded any formal training in design methodology.

ISDi / Universidad de La Habana
2018 — 2021
UI Design Professor
Havana, Cuba / Remote

Taught UI design at the Instituto Superior de Diseño, affiliated with the University of Havana — developing curriculum, facilitating studio sessions, and building shared understanding of complex concepts across students at different levels of ability and experience.

The habits formed here — making the implicit explicit, adjusting communication based on who's in the room, building consensus around ambiguous problems — have proven more transferable to enterprise design work than I would have expected. The same skills that make a good teacher make a good facilitator. I've drawn on them consistently ever since.
