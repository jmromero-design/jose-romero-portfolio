About

I've always been more comfortable
with hard problems than easy briefs.

I'm Jose Romero — a senior product designer with 12 years of experience across digital products, conversion strategy, and design systems. I work in English and Spanish at professional level, and I'm currently based in Zaragoza, Spain, working as an embedded senior designer on IKEA's New Business Platform — part of the team building two peer-to-peer marketplaces from their early stages through European expansion.

Before that, I spent three years at Flat 101, one of Spain's leading digital consultancies, running UX and CRO work across four to eight concurrent enterprise accounts simultaneously. Santander Nordics, Meliá Hotels International, Mapfre, Mutua Madrileña, AXA Spain, IQOS, XTI — different industries, different team structures, often at the same time.

That period built something I didn't fully understand until later: the ability to enter an unfamiliar environment, read what it needs, adapt my role to fit, and move things forward without waiting for a clarity that may never fully arrive.

Independent Studio
2013 — 2019
Design Director / Founder
Havana, Cuba

I started working professionally at 21, while in the third year of a five-year degree in Visual Communication Design, balancing both simultaneously. What began as independent work grew into a studio of five people — covering brand identity, editorial design, digital interfaces, illustration, animation, video production, and marketing, for national-scale clients in Cuba.

Running that studio meant handling more than design: client relationships, team management, supplier negotiations, finances, and new business — across disciplines and under significant resource constraints. The scale was different from anything I've done in Europe, but the operational principles were the same. It gave me a foundation in structured thinking and commercial reality that preceded any formal training in design methodology.

ISDi / Universidad de La Habana
2018 — 2021
UI Design Professor
Havana, Cuba / Remote

Taught UI design at the Instituto Superior de Diseño, affiliated with the University of Havana — developing curriculum, facilitating studio sessions, and building shared understanding of complex concepts across students at different levels of ability and experience.

The habits formed here — making the implicit explicit, adjusting communication based on who's in the room, building consensus around ambiguous problems — have proven more transferable to enterprise design work than I would have expected. The same skills that make a good teacher make a good facilitator. I've drawn on them consistently ever since.
