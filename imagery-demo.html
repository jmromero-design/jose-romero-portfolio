Imagery treatments

Internal demo — four ways to integrate product screenshots into the editorial system. Placeholder UI drawn as SVG; real screenshots drop into the same frames.

Treatment 1 — Editorial pair · cover language

Two staggered screens on a card that reuses the concentric-circle cover visual. Reads as a designed object, not a pasted screenshot. Best as the primary visual near the top of a case study.

Treatment 2 — Full-bleed strip

Edge-to-edge band, same language as the article's full-bleed stripe. Phones staggered at different heights for rhythm. Good as a mid-scroll pacing break inside long case studies.

Treatment 3 — Annotated single

One screen + one margin note in the article's sidenote language. Use when the screenshot exists to prove a specific decision, not to decorate.

Design decision

Progressive disclosure keeps the first decision binary. The highlighted tier is the default — chosen by 72% of users in testing — so the screen carries one choice, not four.

Treatment 4 — Browser frame · desktop products

For Meliá, Santander, and other web work: minimal browser chrome, no fake OS window. Sits directly in the reading column.
